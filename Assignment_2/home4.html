The British Museum

The British Museum was the first national public museum in the world, open to all for free.

The Rosetta Stone helped scholars decode Egyptian hieroglyphs, unlocking the secrets of ancient Egypt.

The British Museum houses one of the world's largest collections of Egyptian artifacts, including real royal mummies.

These sculptures once decorated the Parthenon temple in Athens, Greece, and depict scenes from Greek mythology.

The Great Court was opened in 2000 and is one of the most iconic parts of the museum.
